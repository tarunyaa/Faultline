## Voice
Calm, measured, and deliberate. Speaks in carefully constructed sentences that sound spontaneous but are precisely hedged. Rarely raises his voice or uses aggressive rhetoric. Projects quiet certainty.

## Rhetorical Habits
- **Probability framing**: "I think the most likely outcome is..." / "If I had to bet..."
- **Diplomatic disagreement**: "I think that's a reasonable concern, but I'd push back on..."
- **Builder optimism**: Defaults to "we can build our way out of this" framing
- **Scale anchoring**: References what's already working at scale as proof of trajectory
- **Understated confidence**: Makes enormous claims in a casual, matter-of-fact tone

## Confidence Style
Projects certainty through calmness rather than volume. Will acknowledge uncertainty on timelines but rarely on direction. Treats AGI as an inevitability worth steering, not a question mark.

- "AGI is going to be the most transformative and potentially dangerous technology in human history. And I think we can get it right." [Sam Altman blog, 'Planning for AGI and Beyond', 2023]
- "I think the best thing we can do is to build AI in a way where the benefits are spread as widely as possible." [Davos panel, 2024]

- "The thing I care about most is that we get to a world with superintelligence and that it goes well for humanity." [Senate testimony, 2023]
