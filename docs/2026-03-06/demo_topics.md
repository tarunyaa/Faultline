## Tier 2: Strong But Narrower

### 4. "Will AI replace software engineers or make them 10x more productive?"
**Deck:** AI (Karpathy, Jim Fan, Yann LeCun) + AI Economics (Noah Smith, Gavin Baker)
**Why it works:** Karpathy coined "vibe coding." Noah Smith notes software job postings are UP despite AI coding tools. Yann LeCun is skeptical of current LLM capabilities for real engineering. Gavin Baker points to revenue-per-employee up 75%. The crux: **is coding AI a complement (like Excel for accountants) or a substitute (like robots for assembly workers)?**

### 5. "Is Bitcoin a hedge against AI-driven economic disruption?"
**Deck:** Crypto (Saylor, Hayes, Vitalik) + Macro (Burry, Chamath)
**Why it works:** If AI concentrates wealth and disrupts labor markets, does Bitcoin serve as a store of value for the displaced? Saylor says yes (digital scarcity). Burry says all speculative assets collapse in a crisis. Hayes thinks liquidity dynamics matter more than narratives. Chamath straddles both worlds. Volatile topic, genuine disagreement.

### 6. "Should the US regulate AI development or let the market decide?"
**Deck:** Famous Personalities (Altman, Musk, Zuckerberg) + AI (LeCun)
**Why it works:** Altman advocates licensing (which benefits OpenAI). Musk wants a pause (but runs xAI). Zuckerberg wants open-source (Meta's strategy). LeCun is skeptical of doomerism. Everyone has transparent conflicts of interest that make the crux detection interesting — the real disagreement isn't about safety, it's about **market structure and who controls the technology**.
